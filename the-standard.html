Welcome to the Standard
for Public Code

The Standard for Public Code is a set of criteria that supports public organizations in developing and maintaining software and policy together. Its criteria are aligned with guidelines and best practices of open source software development.

Version 0.7.1 (pre-release)

Published 31 July 2023 on GitHub

Key attributes of the Standard for Public Code:
Provides guidance to public organizations building open source solutions, to increase collaboration and reuse by similar organizations in the public sector in other places.
Includes advice for policy makers, government managers, developers and vendors.
Supports the collaborative creation of codebases that are usable, open, legible, accountable, accessible and sustainable.
Applicable to codebases for all levels of government, from supranational to municipal.

The Standard was accepted as a Digital Public Good for supporting the UN Sustainable Development Goals

Introduction course

Together with Open & Agile Smart Cities we have created a free and open online introduction course to the Standard for Public Code. It’s a self study course and offers a certification of completion to those who finish all the modules.
